Heat Equation

The Heat Equation is a fundamental partial differential equation used to model the distribution of heat (or variation in temperature) in a given region over time . One of its key applications is in thermal analysis of materials and systems, providing insights into the diffusion of heat through a surface or object. In the context of our study, we consider this equation to simulate the diffusion of heat along a domain and observe how the temperature evolves with time under different conditions.

FDM Solvers

The Finite Difference Method is one of several techniques for obtaining numerical solutions to Heat Equation. In all numerical solvers, the continuous PDE is replaced with a discrete approximation.The core idea of the finite difference method is to replace continuous derivatives with difference formulas that involve only the discrete values associated with positions on the mesh.
